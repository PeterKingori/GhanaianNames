Ghanaian Akan Names

Learn what your name is in Ghanaian culture according to the day you were born.

The Akan people of Ghana have a distinct naming system of their children. Newborns are named depending on the day of the week they are born. These names have special meanings as a symbol of their culture, ancestral ties, and even concerning the soul and character of the child. This tradition naming system is also followed in other West African countries with distinct varieties.

Most Ghanaians have a name from this system, as well as an English or Christian name. Prominent people named using this system include the former United Nations Secretrary-General, Koffi Atta Annan, who was named for being born on a Friday. Another notable person is Kwame Nkrumah, Ghana's first persident, named for being born on a Saturday.
